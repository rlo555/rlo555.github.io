Transactional Funding For Real Estate Investors

What Is Transactional Funding?

Transactional funding is a type of short-term financing that is used to fund real estate transactions where a property is bought and sold by an investor on the same day through a double closing (back-to-back closings). The money is typically used for just one day, since both closings (original seller to investor: A-B, and investor to end buyer: B-C) occur the same day.



Why Use Transactional Funding?

Transactional funding is used when a property is bought and sold by an investor on the same day in situations that require double closings to take place. It is especially helpful in the following scenarios:

When closing agents won't allow investors to use the funds provided by the end buyer to close the transaction with the original seller.

When the investor may not have (or may not want to use) their own funds to close the transaction with the original seller.

When the investor doesn't want to go through the slow and expensive loan application process with a traditional bank.

When the original seller is a bank (e.g. short sale and REO properties), that won't allow the investor to use funds provided by the end buyer.

When the investor makes a large profit that may entice the original seller to back out of the deal if they see that profit detailed on the HUD.

Transactional funds can be used to cover the purchase price, closing costs, and other fees, and are reimbursed to the lender the same day the double closing takes place.



How Does Transactional Funding Work?

To apply for transactional funding we need to receive:

  • A-B contract (between you and your seller)
  • B-C contract (between you and your end-buyer)

The other two requisites are that both closings: A-B and B-C, must take place on the same day and with the same closing agent.

FAQ


What are the requirements to request transactional funding?
  • Funding Requirements Agreement (we will send) signed by you.
  • Copy of signed contracts A-B (investor “B” buys from seller “A”) and B-C (investor "B" sale to end buyer “C”).
  • Closing Statements for the A-B and B-C closings minimum 24 hours in advance of funds disbursement.
  • Personal contact information + government issued ID.
  • If applicable, your entity documents (Operating Agreement, Articles of Incorporation/Organization).
  • Full contact information for closing agent including info. for person handling transaction.

Before transactional funds can be disbursed, End-buyer’s (C) funds must have been wired and cleared in closing agent’s escrow before transactional funding can be disbursed. Funds from the end-buyer (“C”) will only be accepted via Domestic Wires only

How long does it take for me to get approved?

Once all our Funding Requirements are met we can approve your funding in as little as 48 hours, as we work with our own funds.

What percentage of the original seller's property price do you fund?

We do 100% funding of the purchase amount balance (i.e. minus earnest/due diligence deposit) + closing costs.

Do I need a legal entity to apply for transactional funding?

No, you don't need one. We provide transactional funding to individuals as well as LLCs and corporations.

Can A-B and B-C closings be done with two different closing agents?

We only fund if both closings are with the same agent.

Can A-B and B-C closings be done in two different days?

We only fund same-day closings.

Do you run a credit check and/or income verification to provide funding?

No, we don't require credit or income verification.

What is the difference between Transactional Funding and Hard Money Lending?

Transactional funding is a short-term (one day) loan extended to a wholesaler to use for closing on the purchase of a property that they are closing with an end buyer on the same day. A hard money loan is extended for more than one day, to purchase a property, but can also be used for renovating and selling the property later on. We provide only transactional funding, not hard money loans.

Are there any special circumstances that I need to be aware of?

While the process to obtain transactional funding is fairly simple and straightforward, there may be occasions in which things don't go as planned. A list of special circumstances and how they are handled will be provided with our Funding Requirements Agreement.
